# Platform limits — what automation can and cannot do

## What App Launcher can automate

After adding a new app:

- Brand name and username suggestions
- Username availability checks (where APIs permit)
- Profile bio, description, banner text
- Profile image and logo concepts
- First 30 posts, hashtags, content calendar
- Link page
- Launch checklist
- Schedule **approved** posts via official APIs where supported

## What it usually cannot do automatically

Major platforms block fully automated account creation (spam prevention):

- Meta (Facebook, Instagram)
- LinkedIn
- X (Twitter)
- Reddit
- TikTok

Account creation typically requires human verification, CAPTCHA, email/phone verification, and ToS acceptance. Bypassing these violates platform rules and risks suspension.

## Launch assistant approach

When you click **Launch New App**:

1. Generate app branding
2. Prepare profile images and banners
3. Generate bios per platform
4. Open official sign-up pages
5. Guide manual verification
6. Connect accounts via official APIs once they exist
7. Generate content, monitor engagement, track performance

## Account strategy

You do not need separate social accounts for every app.

Many indie developers use:

- One **developer brand** account for the portfolio
- Per-app landing pages and store listings
- Dedicated app accounts only when volume justifies it

App Launcher supports both strategies.

## Realistic expectations

- **0 → some sales:** achievable with consistent marketing and tracking
- **0 → thousands/month:** hard; requires time, testing, iterations

Order of operations: **Build → Market → Learn → Improve → Repeat**

The platform helps you test ideas and learn what works — it does not replace demand discovery.
